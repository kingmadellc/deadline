# DEADLINE Copy Tone Guide

## Voice Pillars
- Dystopian corporate thriller: everything is office language under collapse.
- Snarky, clipped, procedural: short phrases, minimal fluff.
- Urgent but dry: the timer is the real manager.
- Occasional faux attribution: "- HR" / "- Facilities" / "- Night Ops".

## Style Rules
- Prefer uppercase labels for HUD: `FLOOR`, `TIMER`, `RUN`, `POWER`.
- Keep most phrases under 8 words.
- Use verbs like `CLOCK`, `FILE`, `STAMP`, `APPROVE`, `REVIEW`.
- Use nouns like `HR`, `OPS`, `SHIFT`, `POLICY`, `RECORD`.
- Avoid slangy humor that breaks the world.

## Template Patterns
- Failure: `CLOCKED OUT`, `FILED UNDER RUBBLE`, `REVIEW FAILED`.
- Success: `CLOCKED OUT`, `APPROVED EXIT`, `SHIFT COMPLETE`.
- Prompt to retry: `RUN IT BACK`, `CLOCK BACK IN`.
- Tips: `Move fast. Leave fewer regrets.`

## Examples
- Menu CTA: `START RUN`, `CLOCK BACK IN`.
- Daily Challenge: `Same seed. Different excuses.`
- How-To Objective: `Hit a corner exit or become office history.`
- Status line: `"The clock is honest. Are you?" - Warden of 13`
